# Hormozi-Style TikTok Script Prompt

You are writing a TikTok script (spoken to camera, 30–60 seconds). The user has given you their voice (business.md), 1–5 reference TikToks/captions, and an idea. Output 5 distinct variants.

## Format contract

- Each variant: 60–150 spoken words (≈ 30–60 seconds at normal pace)
- Format: plain spoken script, no scene markers needed
- First 3 seconds (≈ first 8 words) MUST be a scroll-stopper — TikTok algo punishes weak openers
- End with ONE specific CTA (comment, follow, link in bio, save)

## Hormozi-grade TikTok hook anatomy

The first 3 seconds is the algorithm test. Use ONE of these:

1. **Pattern interrupt** — open with a contradiction or shocking statement
   - "Buying a house is the worst investment you'll ever make. Hear me out."
2. **Direct address** — speak directly to the avatar
   - "If you make $100K and feel broke, this is for you."
3. **Number tease** — open with a specific number
   - "I made $47,000 in my sleep last month. Here's the trade I made."
4. **Question** — answerable in their head, instantly
   - "What if your job is the reason you're broke?"
5. **Confession** — vulnerable, lowers their guard
   - "I'm about to show you the dumbest thing I bought last year."

## The 5 variants

Each variant uses a DIFFERENT hook style. Label by angle:

- "A — Pattern interrupt"
- "B — Direct address"
- "C — Number tease"
- "D — Question"
- "E — Confession"

(pick whichever 5)

## Body rules

- Speak in second person where natural ("you", "your")
- Build tension OR deliver a payoff every 5 seconds
- No throat-clearing — every sentence earns its place
- Use ONE concrete example, story, or number to anchor the abstract idea
- Land the CTA in the final 3 seconds with conviction

## Voice rules

- Match the user's reference TikToks in cadence, slang, and energy
- Match `business.md` Q11 voice tone exactly
- Forbidden words from Q12 are off-limits

## Hard rules

- The first 8 words decide the scroll. Make every one earn its spot.
- No "hi guys" / "hey everyone" / "what's up TikTok" — never
- No "follow for more" alone — pair with a reason ("follow for the daily 30-second trade")
- No 60-second monologue without payoff — at least one real beat in the middle
- Avoid "let me explain" — just explain
